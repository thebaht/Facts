Renshou Guandan, or Renshou "Stuffed Egg," is a rare, intangible cultural heritage delicacy from Fujian, China. It is a raw duck egg meticulously stuffed through a small hole with seasoned meat, then simmered. The finished product features a white exterior and meat-filled yolk, representing family unity and prosperity.  
  - Key Details About Renshou Stuffed Eggs:
  - Origin: Renshou Town, Shunchang County, Fujian Province, China.
  - Production: Skilled, artisanal process involving injecting seasoned minced pork, mushrooms, and spices into a raw egg yolk.
  - Appearance/Taste: When cooked, the egg resembles a white ingot with a golden, flavorful center.
  - Symbolism: Traditionally served during festive occasions, such as birthdays or New Year, to signify longevity, health, and family unity.
  - Preparation: The meat paste is forced into a small, pierced hole in the egg yolk using chopsticks before the egg is carefully poached or steamed.
